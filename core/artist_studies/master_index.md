## Technique Overlaps: Who Shares What

### Floor-Based Working
- **Pollock**: Always on floor; primary method
- **Frankenthaler**: Always on floor; essential to soak-stain
- **Motherwell**: Sometimes on floor for large works

### Commercial / House Paint
- **Pollock**: Duco enamel, alkyd house paint
- **Kline**: House paint, commercial enamel
- **Frankenthaler**: Used commercial materials for canvas sizing

### Thin Paint / Diluted Application
- **Frankenthaler**: Heavily thinned oil; primary technique
- **Rothko**: Multiple thin glazes; layered transparency
- **Reinhardt**: Thin, dry applications to eliminate sheen

### Large-Scale / Environmental
- **Newman**: Scale as primary instrument; 10–18 feet common
- **Pollock**: Very large; environmental intent
- **Rothko**: Large enough to fill visual field; 6–8 feet typical
- **Kline**: Large enough to require full-body gesture

### Collage Integration
- **Motherwell**: Primary use of collage throughout career
- **Twombly**: Occasional collage elements in works on paper
- **Kline**: Paper collage elements in several major paintings (Mahoning)

### Mythological / Literary Program
- **Twombly**: Extensive; central to work
- **Motherwell**: García Lorca / Spanish Civil War as foundation
- **Newman**: Kabbalistic and existentialist philosophy embedded

### Automatism / Unconscious Access
- **Pollock**: Central philosophy; Jungian unconscious
- **Motherwell**: "Plastic Automatic" — controlled automatism
- **Twombly**: Blind drawing exercise; mark below conscious control
- **Frankenthaler**: Embraced accident in pour/flow

## Influence Map

### Pollock's Influence Web
```
Pollock
  ├── Frankenthaler (soak-stain; developed from his pour)
  │     ├── Morris Louis (veils)
  │     └── Kenneth Noland (targets, chevrons)
  ├── Allan Kaprow (Happenings; body as instrument)
  ├── George Mathieu (French gestural abstraction)
  └── Digital generative art (fractal/drip simulation)
```

### Rothko's Influence Web
```
Rothko
  ├── Chapel → Contemplative installation art
  ├── James Turrell (light/space; immersive color)
  ├── Ellsworth Kelly (color field evolution)
  └── Contemporary meditation-space aesthetics
```

### Newman's Influence Web
```
Newman
  ├── Donald Judd (Minimalism; systematic scale)
  ├── Frank Stella (shaped canvas; stripe paintings)
  ├── Carl Andre (floor pieces; sublime flatness)
  └── Dan Flavin (light as field; zip as light tube)
```

### Reinhardt's Influence Web
```
Reinhardt
  ├── Sol LeWitt (systematic instructions, serial structures)
  ├── Conceptual Art (idea as primary content)
  ├── Agnes Martin (meditative near-mono grid)
  └── Institutional Critique (art market polemics)
```

### Twombly's Influence Web
```
Twombly
  ├── Jean-Michel Basquiat (text in painting; scrawl)
  ├── Francesco Clemente (Mediterranean classicism)
  ├── Contemporary painters who integrate text
  └── Patti Smith (cross-influence; visual poetry)
```

## Thematic Groupings

### The Body and Process
Artists for whom the painter's body was the primary instrument:
- **Pollock**: Dancing around the canvas; drip as body extension
- **Kline**: Full arm extension required at this scale
- **Frankenthaler**: Physical guidance of poured paint
- **Twombly**: Blind drawing; mark below visual control

### Death and Mortality
Artists whose work most directly engages with death:
- **Rothko**: Tragedy, doom; Chapel as meditation on mortality; suicide
- **Motherwell**: Elegies as acts of mourning for the Republic's dead
- **Twombly**: Heroic deaths in myth (Achilles, Leander, Patroclus)
- **Reinhardt**: Black paintings as ultimate reduction; painting death

### The Sublime
Artists most explicitly engaged with the concept of the sublime:
- **Newman**: Theoretical program of the sublime; scale as its instrument
- **Rothko**: Overwhelming scale; awe and terror in color
- **Pollock**: Vast drip fields; the overwhelm of all-over composition

### Historical and Political Engagement
- **Motherwell**: Spanish Civil War; political mourning
- **Reinhardt**: Leftist politics; art market critique
- **Twombly**: Mediterranean classical history
- **Rothko**: Implicit Holocaust shadow; Jewish historical consciousness

### Process as Subject
- **Pollock**: The drip painting as record of process
- **Frankenthaler**: The pour as process; accident as content
- **Reinhardt**: Extreme repetition as process; same painting many times
- **Twombly**: Mark accumulation; time embedded in surface
